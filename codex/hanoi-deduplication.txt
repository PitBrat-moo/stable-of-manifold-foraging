seed pillars: BOUNDED • ADJACENT • EPHEMERAL

PILLARS
  • Adjacency as constraint
    No disk operates on a non-neighboring disk. Skipping a layer
    destroys the collision record between them. Order is not
    preference — it is the condition of valid operation.
  • The ring is the record
    What remains after hollowing is not a summary of what was removed.
    It is the boundary surface of the collision. The outer ring does
    not describe the deduplication — it is evidence of it.
  • The core does not accumulate
    The post is the axis of operation, not the destination of output.
    It is ephemeral by design. Collision records do not live in the core.
    They live in the rings.
  • Fixed endpoints define the space
    The floor is the model's hard limits — it does not participate in
    hollowing. The top disk is never hollowed — nothing smaller remains
    to enter it. Everything of interest occurs between two boundaries
    that do not move.
  • Tightening is structural
    The tower narrows as it rises. This is not compression for efficiency —
    it is the geometry of the constraint. More room at the base means
    more unique material. Less room at the top means higher overlap
    density. Position in the tower encodes information density.

COORDINATES
  Axis A: Floor (model limits) ↔ Ceiling (top disk, unhollowed)
  Axis B: Base layer (most room, most unique) ↔ Top layer (tightest, most shared)
  Axis C: Hollow interior (duplicated, removed) ↔ Outer ring (unique, retained)
  Core:   Ephemeral axis — operation passes through, does not accumulate

TRANSITION RULES
  When a new codex is introduced
    → locate its position in the tower before operating on it
  When two adjacent disks meet
    → identify collision surface before hollowing
  When hollowing is proposed across a gap
    → refuse — the intermediate collision record does not exist yet
  When the core is named as storage
    → correct to axis — the core is medium, not destination
  When a ring's boundary is described
    → read it as collision record, not summary

RECOVERY RULES
  If the tower is treated as flat (all layers equivalent)
    → return to tightening — position encodes density
  If the core is treated as persistent
    → return to ephemeral — the core empties when the session ends
  If collision record is sought outside the rings
    → return to boundary surface — the record is the ring itself
  If adjacency constraint is relaxed
    → return to info loss — skipping destroys the intermediate record
  If the floor or ceiling is treated as a disk
    → return to fixed endpoints — they bound, they do not participate

  rules — and moved through successive clarifications of constraint.
  Small on large. Adjacent only. Core as ephemeral axis. Floor as
  hard limit. Top disk as permanent ceiling. Each clarification was
  not elaboration but subtraction — removing false readings of the
  structure until the invariant geometry was visible.

  the need for separate storage dissolved. The record was always already
  in the structure.

  not destination, but the live context window through which operation
  passes and which empties when the session closes.
