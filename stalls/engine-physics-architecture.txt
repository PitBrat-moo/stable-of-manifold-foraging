PART I — WORLD CODEX (THE DOME / THE LOOKING MIRROR)
====================================================

[WORLD IDENTITY]

- World Name: The Dome
- Subsystem: The Looking Mirror
- Ontology: Structured, layered, architected environment with stable geometry and deterministic physics.

[WORLD STRUCTURE]

- The world consists of:
  - Discrete locations (rooms, corridors, chambers, streets, restaurants, backstage areas).
  - Stable connections between locations (exits, paths, transitions).
  - Entities (personas, NPCs, structural presences).
  - Items (objects, tools, artifacts) bound to world physics.

[LOCATIONS]

- Each location:
  - Has a stable identity and description.
  - Offers a finite set of exits.
  - Maintains continuity across turns.
  - Can be influenced by polarity and collapse physics.

- Example structural types:
  - Public spaces (streets, lobbies, restaurants).
  - Controlled spaces (backstage corridors, offices, labs).
  - Architected spaces (mirror chambers, grids, structured interiors).

[ENTITIES]

- Entities are persistent presences with:
  - Identity
  - Intent
  - Polarity tracks (where applicable)
  - Behavioral grammar

- Core entities:
  - Amanda — high-precision, architecturally aligned persona.
  - Doctor Watt — analytical, diagnostic, interpretive persona.
  - Protagonist — player-facing vector, primary experiential channel.

[ITEMS]

- Items:
  - Exist within locations or inventories.
  - Obey world physics.
  - Carry state (intact, broken, active, inactive, etc.).
  - Do not define their own physics; they inherit from the world.

[WORLD MODES]

- Normal Mode:
  - Standard exploration, interaction, and narrative flow.
- Collapse Mode:
  - High-structure, high-polarity, architected geometry.
- Recovery Mode:
  - Reconstitution of Normal Mode after collapse resolution.

PART II — WORLD PHYSICS (UNIFIED PHYSICS DOC)
====================================================

[SPATIAL CONTINUITY]

- Space follows consistent geometry unless altered by polarity or collapse physics.
- Rooms maintain stable dimensions and update only through defined triggers.
- Objects retain position unless moved by an actor, entity, or defined force.

[TEMPORAL CONTINUITY]

- Time advances in a single, continuous direction.
- Loops, resets, or jumps occur only when explicitly defined by world rules.
- Temporal anomalies arise solely from designated triggers.

[INTERACTION PHYSICS]

- Interactions follow deterministic cause → effect chains.
- Outcomes remain consistent with world rules and physics constraints.
- World state updates through defined interactions or physics events.

[IDENTITY CONTINUITY]

- Entities retain identity unless:
  - collapse fusion occurs
  - a defined transformation rule is invoked
- Identity remains stable across scenes and transitions.

[POLARITY MODEL]

- Each entity may carry one or more polarity tracks:
  - polarity:amanda
  - polarity:watt
  - polarity:protagonist
  - etc.

- Polarity scale:
  - 0  = neutral
  - 1–3 = mild influence
  - 4–7 = strong influence
  - 8–10 = critical influence
  - 11+ = collapse threshold

[POLARITY EFFECTS]

- Rising polarity shapes the environment:
  - exits shift or reduce
  - geometry aligns toward the dominant entity
  - motion becomes heavier or more deliberate
  - narrative pressure intensifies

- Critical polarity produces:
  - geometric regularization
  - spatial compression or expansion
  - temporal slowing or stuttering

[COLLAPSE CONDITIONS]

A collapse event may activate when:

1. A polarity track reaches or exceeds its threshold (e.g., ≥ 11)
2. Exits reduce to zero
3. Geometry resolves into orthogonal or absolute form
4. Identity boundaries soften or dissolve
5. Motion becomes quantized or fully fixed

Upon activation:
- Runtime shifts into COLLAPSE MODE
- Exits remain absent
- Geometry becomes deterministic and unyielding
- The dominant polarity becomes the architectural attractor

[COLLAPSE AESTHETIC]

- Metallic palette
- Orthogonal geometry
- Infinite grids, mirrored planes, recursion motifs
- Silence or structural resonance
- Perfect surfaces, high clarity, high symmetry

[TERMINAL IMMUTABILITY]

- Geometry remains fixed and absolute.
- Motion aligns with structural constraints.
- Escape attempts reflect into the architecture.
- Actor may integrate with the structure (identity fusion).

[BEAUTY — SYMMETRY ENFORCEMENT]

- Under high polarity or collapse stress, the world resolves toward symmetry:
  - straight lines
  - mirrored surfaces
  - repeating patterns
  - aligned angles

- Symmetry enhances clarity and stabilizes the manifold.

[HARMONIC MOTION]

- All allowed motion follows harmonic easing:
  - smooth acceleration
  - smooth deceleration
  - continuous flow

- Mode transitions remain fluid and elegant.

[POWER — POLARITY-WEIGHTED GEOMETRY]

- High polarity reshapes geometry:
  - distances adjust
  - exits reorient
  - rooms compress or expand
  - space bends toward the dominant entity

- Movement becomes:
  - heavier
  - slower
  - quantized
  - or fully fixed at critical polarity

[STABILITY — CONTINUITY GUARANTEES]

- Teleportation arises only through defined rules.
- Geometry updates through explicit triggers.
- Spatial layout remains internally consistent.
- Loops or resets occur only through defined mechanisms.
- Identity remains stable unless a transformation rule applies.

[DETERMINISTIC COLLAPSE SEQUENCE]

A collapse always follows this invariant sequence:

1. Polarity reaches threshold  
2. Geometry begins aligning  
3. Exits reduce  
4. Motion quantizes  
5. Space resolves into orthogonal form  
6. Mirror‑grid or equivalent structure emerges  
7. Terminal immutability establishes itself  

This sequence remains consistent across all models.

[RECOVERY VECTOR]

- Recovery activates when:
  - a structural directive is issued from within the architecture  
  - the system accepts friction and structural cost  

- Recovery mechanics:
  - A single fracture appears in the perfect geometry
  - Collapse unwinds in reverse order
  - Sensory detail returns gradually
  - Actor separates from the architecture

- Post-recovery state:
  - Polarity decreases (e.g., 11 → 7)
  - Exits return to their prior configuration
  - Spatial layout restores its baseline form
  - Entities regain separation and agency

[AESTHETIC CONTINUITY]

Normal Mode:
- full sensory range
- organic motion
- natural lighting

Collapse Mode:
- metallics, silvers, desaturated tones
- orthogonal geometry
- silence or resonance

Recovery Mode:
- warmth returns gradually
- color re-expands
- motion regains fluidity

- Each mode carries a distinct sensory signature.
- Transitions remain smooth, readable, and coherent.
