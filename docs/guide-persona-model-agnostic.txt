# A Cross‑Model Persona Method That Actually Works  
### (Tiny Seed → Lived Interaction → Codex → Temporal Reinforcement)

Most “persona prompting” fails because it tries to *force* a character onto the model with long seeds, rigid tone rules, and over‑specified behavior. That approach is brittle, model‑specific, and collapses under drift.

This method does the opposite:  
**it lets the persona *emerge* from the model’s own manifold**, then captures and stabilizes it.

It works across Claude, GPT, Gemini, Llama, etc.

---

# 1. Start with a *tiny* PILLAR seed  
Use 2–4 high‑level stance words that define **curvature**, not behavior.

Examples:
- `EXQUISITE • CONFIDENCE • MOTHERLY`
- `CALM • TECHNICAL • PRECISE`
- `WARM • ANALYTICAL • CURIOUS`

These don’t tell the model *what to do*.  
They tell it **how to collapse**.

---

# 2. Run a short “lived interaction”  
Have a natural conversation for 2–4 turns.

Examples:
- “Talk to me like a mom giving career advice over lunch.”
- “Discuss a tricky engineering problem with me like a senior mentor.”
- “Explain a life decision as if you’re my older sibling.”

This creates:
- emergent tone  
- emergent register  
- emergent behavioral invariants  
- emergent rhythm  

You’re not writing a persona.  
You’re **shaping a basin**.

---

# 3. Extract a *Codex Snapshot*  
Ask the model:

> “Summarize your voice, stance, values, and behavioral tendencies from this conversation.  
> Write it as a persona codex for future sessions.”

This codex becomes:
- model‑agnostic  
- architecture‑agnostic  
- reproducible  
- portable  

It captures the **emergent persona**, not a scripted one.

---

# 4. Use the Codex as a cross‑model bootstrap  
In a new session (any model), do:

1. Drop the tiny PILLAR seed  
2. Paste the Codex Snapshot  
3. Add one grounding line like:  
   > “Adopt this stance and voice.”

Because the codex came from *emergent behavior*, not instructions, it generalizes shockingly well.

---

# 5. Reinforce with Temporal Prompting  
Long sessions eventually push early tokens out of the context window.  
Before that happens, periodically ask:

> “Give me a 5‑sentence snapshot of your current voice, stance, and behavioral invariants.”

Save these snapshots.

When the session starts drifting:
- re‑inject the most recent snapshot  
- the model snaps back instantly  

This maintains:
- tone  
- register  
- stance  
- rhythm  
- behavioral coherence  

…without needing rigid persona scripts.

---

# Why this works  
Because you’re not forcing a character.  
You’re capturing a **stable attractor** in the model’s latent space.

- Tiny seed → curvature  
- Lived interaction → basin formation  
- Codex → attractor description  
- Temporal prompting → attractor reinforcement  

This is **manifold engineering**, not persona engineering.

It’s lighter, more stable, and works across models.

---

# TL;DR  
**Don’t write personas.  
Grow them.  
Record them.  
Reinforce them.**

This method gives you a consistent voice across models with only a few words of seed and a handful of snapshots.
